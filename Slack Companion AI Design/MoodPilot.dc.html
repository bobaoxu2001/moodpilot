01 · Product one-liner
A context-aware emotional AI companion that helps you understand emotionally loaded messages and craft the right reply — in work, school, dating, and the job hunt.
03 · Core user problem
Hard messages create a spike of anxiety and a blank cursor. People fire off something they regret, over-apologize, or stall for hours. They don't lack words — they lack a clear read of the situation and a fast way to sound like themselves under pressure.
02 · Target users & use cases
Early-career professionals
Navigating managers, feedback, and Slack politics for the first time.
Job seekers & students
Rejection emails, recruiter outreach, professor feedback, networking DMs.
Anxious texters & daters
Ambiguous tone, ghosting, awkward asks where the right words matter.
Tense Slack thread Job rejection Manager feedback Awkward DM Dating app reply Setting a boundary
04 · Product principles
01
Understand before you advise
Always name the emotion and intent first. No reply without a read.
02
You hold the pen
MoodPilot drafts; the user edits and sends. Nothing is automatic.
03
Sound like you, not like AI
Tone is learned and tunable; replies stay specific and human.
04
Calm, never clinical
Warm and grounding — explicitly not therapy or diagnosis.
05
Memory is a gift, not a default
Personalization is opt-in, transparent, and erasable.
06
Fast to useful
From "ugh, this message" to a draft worth sending in under 15 seconds.

12 · AI output framework
1. What's happening — emotion + intensity, in 1–2 warm sentences.
2. Not-about-you check — separate their stress from your fault.
3. What you want — 2–3 likely goals for your reply.
4. Options — 5 tones, each a send-ready draft.
5. Next step — reply, save, remind, follow up.

13 · Privacy & safety UX
⛉ Granular opt-in memory toggles — on only with consent
⛉ "Why am I seeing this?" on every read
⛉ One-tap forget on each learned preference
⛉ On-device language; no training on your messages
⛉ Never auto-sends — user taps to use a draft
⛉ Crisis-language detector → gentle pointer to real human resources
14 · Non-goals & compliance
✕ Not therapy, counseling, or crisis care
✕ No mental-health diagnosis or treatment claims
✕ Not a health record; not HIPAA-scoped data
✕ Doesn't label users with conditions or moods
✕ No automated sending or impersonation
Positioned as a communication companion, full stop.

19 · Why this matters for Consumer AI Experience

The hard part of consumer AI isn't the model — it's the moment of trust right before someone acts on what it says.

◔
Human–AI interaction, not a chatbot
MoodPilot has a fixed, legible response shape (read → intent → options → action). Users learn to trust it because it behaves the same way every time.
✎
Product taste is the moat
Five tones, a serif "human voice", restraint on data slop — the difference between a feature and a product people love is judgment, not parameters.
⛉
Trust & safety as UX, not legalese
Opt-in memory, "never auto-sends", and clear non-goals (not therapy) are designed into the surface — where consumer AI actually earns or loses permission.
⌥
Bridges design ↔ engineering
The AI output framework doubles as a system prompt + UI contract — one artifact a designer, a PM, and an engineer can all build from.
